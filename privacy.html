D-deskcal Privacy Policy

Last updated: August 22, 2025

This Privacy Policy describes how D-deskcal (the "App") handles your data.

1. No-Server Principle

D-deskcal does not use any developer-operated servers. All your information is transmitted and stored only between Google's servers and your personal computer. No data is ever sent to or stored by the developer.

2. Information We Access and Why

The App requests access to the following information via the Google OAuth 2.0 protocol. This is essential for providing the App's core functionality.

  • Google Calendar Data (Read, Write, Modify, Delete permissions): This permission is used solely for the App's core function: to display your calendar on the screen and to allow you to add, edit, and delete events directly from the App.
  • Basic User Information (Email Address): This is used only to identify which account is currently logged into the App.

3. Where Your Information Is Stored

All data used by the App is stored exclusively in secure locations on your personal computer.

  • Authentication Token (`token.json`): The authentication token required to access Google Calendar is stored only on your local computer. This file is never transmitted outside the App.
  • App Settings and Cache Data (`settings.json`, `calendar.db`): Your configured options and calendar cache data for faster loading are also stored only on your local computer.

4. Sharing of Information

The App does not share or sell any of your information with any third parties.
